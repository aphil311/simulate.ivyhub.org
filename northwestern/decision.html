March 24, 2020

Dear John:

The admission committee has concluded its evaluation of applicants to Northwestern University. Unfortunately, we are unable to offer you a place in the first-year class.

This year, we received over 40,000 applications for an entering class of roughly 2,000 students. Given the relatively small size of the incoming class and the strength of the applicant pool, we cannot admit all of the qualified applicants who apply to Northwestern. As such, you should not feel our decision reflects negatively on you or your potential as a college student. We simply are unable to admit all of the students who present a solid record of achievement.

The task of selecting a class is a challenging one for the admission committee. We carefully reviewed every piece of information you submitted with your application. Unfortunately, we cannot provide explanations about individual admission decisions; however, A Statement from the Dean, linked to this letter, may answer some of your questions about our selection process.

The admission committee appreciates the time and effort you put into applying to Northwestern. We wish you every success as you pursue your education.

Sincerely,

 

 

 

Christopher Watson
Associate Vice President &
Dean of Undergraduate Enrollment

 

Elisabeth Kinsley
Associate Dean &
Director of Undergraduate Admission
